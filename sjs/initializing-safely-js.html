Initializing safely.js

The Safely object can be initialized once safely.js has been included on the page (and has loaded). Using your public key, and providing the options you'd like, you can initialize a Safely object to gain access to its methods.

Initializing the Safely object
const safely = Safely('pk_12kj2ga51JF51jkAj', {
    paymentMethods: ['ACH', 'safely', 'credit', 'mobile_wallet']
});

Safely public key​

The Safely public key is issued by Safely Finance for use in client-side environments. This key is tied to your organization. It must be provided to initialize safely.js before you can begin making calls.

Options​

Attributes in bold are required

AttributeTypeDescription
paymentMethodslistA list of accepted payment methods to present to the user (see Payment Methods)

Payment Methods​

The accepted payment methods that will be presented to the user. These must be approved for your account. If you are interested in adding a payment method that is not currently activated on your account. Get in touch with our team.

One or more of the following must be included when initializing the Safely object:

  • ACH - payment via bank linking and bank transfer
  • safely - pay over time options with Safely Finance
  • credit - credit or debit card payment
  • mobile_wallet - Apple Pay and Google Pay (where available)
